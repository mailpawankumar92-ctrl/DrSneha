Secondary services

Anxiety, depression and related concerns

Anxiety

Support for worry, avoidance, overthinking, social anxiety, panic and stress that may overlap with ADHD.

Depression and low mood

Care for low motivation, shame, exhaustion, hopelessness and mood changes that can follow chronic overwhelm.

Burnout and work stress

Therapy for professionals managing focus demands, workplace pressure, conflict or recovery from psychological injury.

Relationships and family

Support for communication, conflict cycles, emotional load and family patterns where ADHD is part of the picture.

Process

What to expect

The process is designed to feel clear from the beginning, especially if you already feel overloaded.

Step 1 - Enquiry and fit

Share what you are looking for: ADHD assessment, therapy, support for a child or adolescent, or help with related symptoms.

Step 2 - Assessment or therapy plan

Dr. Goyal clarifies goals, gathers relevant history and recommends an evidence-based pathway.

Step 3 - Clarity and support

You receive feedback, recommendations and a practical plan for treatment, accommodations or ongoing therapy where appropriate.
